    Course Description

    This course investigates "how media thinks" through hands-on engagement with the fundamentals of media making. Students learn to create media individually and collaboratively while situating and evaluating their work within contemporary industry and academic contexts. Students develop technical skills alongside theoretical knowledge about media-making through an iterative model: a cyclical process of theorising, making, and reflecting.

    Sessions include a combination of tutorials, lectures, demonstrations, practical activities, and peer-review. The course begins with key components of screen media across fiction and non-fiction forms, including cinematography, video editing techniques and functions, and writing for visual media. Later sessions explore audio-based storytelling, sound recording, sound design, and sonic perspectives, examining how different media forms construct meaning and engage audiences within contemporary media futures and institutions.

    The course emphasises the integration of making and thinking, craft and concept, encouraging students to develop both practical proficiency and critical understanding of media as cultural practice. Students undertake four assessments that scaffold the development of their technical skills and media literacy: a mini media medley comprising four experimental video exercises that introduce fundamental techniques; a video portrait that combines interview, b-roll, and archival footage to create a short documentary piece; a collaborative audio work in podcast form that critically engages with contemporary media debates; and ongoing written reflective practice that theoretically situate their media-making experiments within course readings and concepts.
