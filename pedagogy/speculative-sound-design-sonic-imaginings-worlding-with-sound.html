    Speculative Sound Design: Sonic imaginings and worlding with sound

    Bachelor of Communication (Media), RMIT University AU

    Studio Leader (Tutor), Studio Curriculum Design | July - October 2022

    Course Description

    What are the possibilities for sound design and speculative thinking to open toward multispecies understandings and alternative worlds? How can sound design allow us to build speculative worlds that communicate alternative possibilities?

    Through themes of extinction, ecology, deep time and futures, this studio offers an opportunity to explore the process of designing sounds and the creation of speculative sonic worlds. In this studio students develop practical skills in recording, arranging and manipulating audio, gaining hands-on experience in designing sound for a variety of contexts such as screens, browsers, cinema, walking, performance and three-dimensional spaces.

    In the first half of the semester students are introduced to a series of speculative scenarios and provocations through three experiments in speculative sound design: designing nuclear waste warning signals for future generations, creating vocalisations for extinct animals, and completing sound design for sci-fi film scenes. These exercises build technical competencies while exploring sound's communicative and temporal dimensions. Students subsequently develop a speculative soundwalk that explores environmental ambience and spatial sound design. In the second half of the semester students work collaboratively to construct a comprehensive speculative sonic world, with the aim of imagining new environments, social structures, and possible futures. The course concludes with reflective practice that contextualises their learning through public exhibition and peer engagement across studios.

    Students engage in experiments in thinking, writing, listening, and acoustic communication, drawing on a host of disciplines from critical design and literary studies to ecological criticism, music, field recording and audio cultures. Through workshops that centre playfulness, experimentation and critical thinking, students examine examples across speculative fiction, independent video games, screenplays, soundwalking and sound arts to consider the function of sound within the popular imaginary and alternative futures.
