Amias Hanley

    To Be At Sea: Making site-responsive media in uncertain times

    Bachelor of Communication (Media), RMIT University AU

    Studio Leader (Tutor), Studio Curriculum Design | March - June 2022

    Course Description

    How can we use time-based media to creatively respond to the uncertainty of our times? This studio begins by questioning what constitutes a site and catalyst for media making—a body, river mouth, landfill, discord channel, building, event, bird's nest, or something else entirely. This investigation foregrounds attunement as one of the most valuable skills in contemporary media practice: the ability to sense, respond, and interpret the worlds we inhabit.

    Students explore techniques for practicing attunement through time-based media—audio, video, image, text, digital media, installation and browser-based technologies. Drawing on examples from video works, games design, listening exercises and soundwalks, they experiment with reimagining relations to everyday sites and develop skills for producing media that carries cultural significance and ecological value.

    The studio culminates in a major site-responsive project that demonstrates students' developing sensibilities as media practitioners. Through this work, students aim to create media that prompt sensations of discovery and interdependence within themselves and their audiences. Together we reflect on our roles as media makers at a discombobulated moment in time—questioning what if, rather than forging ahead with narratives of progress, we float, in the open ocean, allowing ourselves to be present and suspended, exploring whether our current moment calls for cultivating an awareness of drifting and uncertainty.
