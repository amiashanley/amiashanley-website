    Sound Futures: eco-techno approaches to sound and listening

    Bachelor of Communication (Media), RMIT University AU

    Studio Leader (Tutor), Studio Curriculum Design | March - June 2023

    Course Description

    Co-led with Joel Stern, this studio questions, how do creative sound practices and critical listening inform our future relations with environments, machines, and more-than-humans? The course positions sound as both mnemonic device and speculative tool, exploring how sonic practices can trigger "future echoes" and open pathways to possible worlds.

    Built around an eco-techno approach to sound and listening, the studio mobilises ecological frameworks alongside investigations into machine listening to examine contemporary complexities—from the data-driven logics of AI and platform capitalism to emerging climate realities. Students develop both theoretical understanding and practical competencies, gaining hands-on experience with audio recording processes, post-production techniques, and sound design fundamentals using digital audio workstations.

    Initial sessions introduce sound, speech, listening, and communication as sites of multi-species expression, sociality, and resistance—examining how sonic practices function as forms of agency and identity. Students then engage in four progressive assignments: creative investigations into machine listening and how devices analyse, respond to, and mis/recognise human speech; speculative soundwalks constructing future environmental ambiences; collaborative sound futures exploring eco-technological relations and alternative world-making; and reflective practice contextualising learning through exhibition outcomes.

    Through this integration of speculative thinking, ecological awareness, and hands-on sound production, students develop capacities to use creative sound practices as tools for imagining and prototyping alternative environmental and technological futures.
