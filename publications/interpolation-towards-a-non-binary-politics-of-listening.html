    Interpolation: Towards a non-binary politics of listening

    Hanley, A. (2025). Interpolation: Towards a non-binary politics of listening. In C. Bartsch, A. Schürmer, & J. Spieker (Eds.), Nonbinär! (Musik and Gender, Vol. 17, pp. 23–26). Georg Olms Verlag. https://doi.org/10.5771/9783487171746-23

    Abstract

    This chapter explores listening as an interpolative and non-binary practice, asking how sound mediates relations between bodies, identities, technologies, and environments. Through a series of reflexive prompts, it considers how non-binary and posthuman subjects might be sounded not through fixed representation, but through ambiguity, relationality, and transformation. Attending to the material, political, and infrastructural conditions of sound, the text frames listening as a situated and contested act, shaped by power, mediation, and ecological entanglement. In doing so, it proposes sonic practice as a mode of resistance and re-imagining across human and non-human worlds.

    Keywords

    Listening, non-binary, sound studies, posthumanism, technology, queer ecologies, sonic politics
