    Aisles of Mimetica: Tracing the Role of Acoustic Mimicry Across Species and Systems

    Hanley, A. (2025). Aisles of Mimetica: Tracing the role of acoustic mimicry across species and systems. In S. Hertz (Ed.), Issue 02: In the Midst of Echoes: Essays on the Turbulences of Listening. High Pitch Magazine.

    Abstract

    This essay traces acoustic mimicry across ecological, cultural, and technological systems, with particular attention to the ultrasonic exchanges that have shaped sixty million years of moth–bat relations. Moving from deep-time auditory evolution and the mechanisms of mimicry to the emergence of ultrasonic jamming as a unique form of sonic interference, the text reframes these relations beyond dominant warfare metaphors, examining how militarised narratives have constrained scientific and cultural understandings of interspecies behaviour.

    Through this critique, the essay positions acoustic mimicry—and jamming in particular—as a mode of adaptive resistance that unsettles anthropocentric accounts of evolution and invites alternative models of multispecies sonic relation. Written as a companion to Aisles of Mimetica, the coda situates these conceptual threads within the installation's methodological use of mimicry and interference.

    Keywords

    Acoustic mimicry, Ultrasonic jamming, Biosonar and echolocation, Multispecies listening, Mimetic machines, Evolutionary relationality, Militarised metaphors, Intra-action.
