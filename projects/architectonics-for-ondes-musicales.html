Amias Hanley

    Architectonics for Ondes Musicales

    8.2 channel audio composition, 30 minutes

    Using ultra-sensitive contact microphones designed for measuring seismic vibrations, this composition captures the acoustic energy resonating through The Substation’s architecture and surrounding infrastructure. The recordings reveal a haunting auditory impression of the landscape and the building’s spectral presence.

    Through this performance the building’s sonic character and surroundings are brought into conversation with selected synthesisers and electronic instruments from MESS’ historic collection, notably the Ondes Musicales. Items from the collection have been chosen for their expressive qualities and timbral synergy with the source material, and are used to voice and accentuate themes of transition and hauntology that unfold in the work.

    Distant rhythms of the adjacent railway, slight resonance of air flow through the ventilation ducts and the illusory melodies of the oil pipeline contouring the east-facing wall, are diffused across eight discrete channels inviting impressions of the environment to emerge through the architecture and building's multi-purpose existence.

    2022 The Substation, Melbourne AU
