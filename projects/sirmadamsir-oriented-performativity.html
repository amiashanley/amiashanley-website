Amias Hanley

    Sirmadamsir: Oriented Performativity

    Or what does judy want?

    2-channel audio work, 7 minutes

    This project explores the intersections of bodies, geographies, and digital technologies, rethinking how identity, gender, and desire are shaped by our interactions with speech-based recognition software and systems. Through sound, voice, and experimental recording techniques, the work examines the dissolution of binary distinctions—inside and outside, human and machine—and the ways these constructs inform our understanding of self and space.

    Blending synthetic voices, recordings made during summer in New York City, and iterative digital-to-analogue playback methods, the piece highlights the tactile and transformative properties of listening. It features an electric piano composition repeatedly re-recorded between digital and cassette formats, creating looping tonal drifts and a melodic terrain that resists linearity.

    the work interrogates how our bodies and desires orient towards digital tools, reshaping performative behaviours and societal structures. It questions systems of power, categorisation, and the markers historically used to verify humanity. By reflecting on the role of machine listening in redefining human identity, the piece speculates on the potential for recognition software to move beyond fixed identity markers and towards broader inclusivity.
